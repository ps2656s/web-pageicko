OUR MONUMENT

Taj Mahal

The Taj Mahal is an ivory-white marble mausoleum on south bank of the Yamuna river in the Indian city of Agra.It was commissioned in 1632 by the Mughal emperor,Shah Jahan (reigned from 1628 to 1658), to house the tomb of his favourite wife,Mumtaz Mahal.

Qutub Minar

The QutbMinar is inspired by the Minaret of Jam in Afghan,Important example of early Afghan architecture,which later evolved into Indo-Islamic Architecture.The Qutb Minar is 72.5 m(239 ft)high,making it the tallest minaret in the world built of bricks.

Sun Temple Konark

On the shores of the Bay of Bengal, bathed in the rays of the rising sun, the temple at Konarak is a monumental representation of the sun god Surya's chariot; its 24 wheels are decorated with symbolic designs and it is led by a team of six horses.

Hawa Mahal

The Hawa Mahal is dedicated to Lord Krishna. It is said that the shape of the building resembles the crown of Krishna. More than a palace, the Hawa Mahal is also a cultural and architectural marvel that reflects a trulY Islamic Mughal architectural styles.

OUR Museum

National Museum Delhi

The National Museum in New Delhi, also known as the National Museum of India, is one of the largest museums in India. Established in 1949, it holds a variety of articles ranging from pre-historic era to modern works of art..

Indian Museum kolkota

The Indian Museum in Central Kolkata, West Bengal, India, also referred to as the Imperial Museum of Calcutta in colonial-era texts, is the ninth oldest museum in the world, the oldest and largest museum in India as well as in Asia.

Museum Chennai

The Government Museum, Chennai, or the Madras Museum, is a museum of human history and culture located in the Government Museum Complex in the neighbourhood of Egmore in Chennai.

Salar-Jung
Museum

The Salar Jung Museum is an art museum located at Dar-ul-Shifa, on the southern bank of the Musi River in the city of Hyderabad, Telangana, India. It is one of the notable National Museums of India.
